The Rise of the Midtier in Super Smash Bros. Melee

By Thomas Manning

The era of the midtier is upon us. Competitive Super Smash Bros. Melee has been an active and thriving scene for over 20 years, and through that time a variety of players have succeeded with character picks outside of the standard meta. Players like Axe, aMSa and Shroomed have reached the highest levels of competition using characters outside the established top 8 picks, but in the post-pandemic era the number of players reaching these levels easily eclipses any previous period of the game's lifespan. As a result, I will be documenting the rise of various mid-to-high tier characters in the past 5 years, and discussing the possible causes.

Donkey Kong

The character most associated with the mid tier renaissance is most certainly Donkey Kong. Historically on the lower end of the mid tier, Donkey Kong was not seen as nearly as much of a threat prior to the rise of players like Junebug, BING, Akir, and ckyulmiqnudaetr (pronounced Kyle McNudater) in the last several years. Each of these players showed strong potential with other characters, but rose quickly up the ranks due to Donkey Kong's consistent and strong advantage state and a combined effort of the Donkey Kong community to optimize defensive flowcharts. While many of these players have since stepped back from competing, the recent emergence of Colorado prodigy Dial M through his Cinderella run at Supermajor Genesis X3 to 17th place inspires confidence in the future of the character.

Pikachu

While less apparent to many due to the previous success of top 10 mainstay Axe, Pikachu has also seen a resurgence in top level representation. Prior to 2020, there was never a Pikachu main outside of Axe in the top 75, and in many years Axe was the only one in the top 100. However, in the modern era, the number of players in the top 100 (and even top 50) has dramatically increased. With players like Swift, OkayP, and Jchu emerging on the scene, the chances of fighting a top level Pikachu player at any given event has become far more likely.

Luigi

The most recent character to become a threat is Luigi, with the charge being led almost entirely by one player: Dylan "RapMonster" Do. Emerging from the same region as the current world number one player Zain, RapMonster has made his recent claim to fame by repeatedly defeating the world's best player in disrespectful and flashy ways. Beating Zain 3 times in extended sets, including in tournament at the year's largest tournament, has catapulted RapMonster and Luigi to the very top level of the game.

Why?

There are many possible reasons for the increase in mid level representation, but most believe the primary force behind it is the increased access to practice tools and learning resources in the modern era. The immediate access to competitive matches with others online thanks to the Slippi client makes practicing midtiers far easier, as the barrier to playing real people used to be much higher. The introduction of training tools such as the Community Edition Training Mode helped develop optimized gameplans that previously would've required far more effort.

Whatever the cause, players are forced to learn the midtier matchups they used to be able to avoid, making the landscape of tournaments scarier today for any emerging top players.
